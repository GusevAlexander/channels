Structure of the experiment

The experiment consists of {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds. Each round has the following stages:

Hiring stage

Each employer can make a wage offer on a public platform and each worker can accept one of these offers. Once an offer is accepted, the respective employer and worker are matched for the remaining stages of a round. All the hiring results are reported: how many were hired and at what wages.

Work stage

Each hired worker has 5 minutes to work on a real effort task. The results are known by the worker and the employer hiring, and resulting payoffs are calculated.

See the detailed instructions explaining the two parts of the experiment:

Hiring stage

The hiring stage lasts at most 2 minutes. Each employer can post a wage offer on the public platform. The offer must be between 30 and 100 points, in intervals of 5, and it can be updated while not accepted but cannot be cancelled entirely.

A worker can accept any one of the standing offers. An accepted offer is removed from the platform and the respective worker and employer are pair up for the rest of the round.

If an offer is not accepted within the 2 minute time window, the employer is not able to hire anyone. Without a contract, workers and employers will not actively participate in remaining stages in a round, but will start again in the next round.

If left without a contract, an employer earns 0 points and a worker earns 20 points. {% if Constants.num_employers > Constants.num_workers %} There will be at least one unemployed worker every round. {% endif %}

If the employer hires a worker, the employer gets 40 points. The wage is reduced from the 40 points and any potential revenue from the work tasks that round. Worker's payoff consist of the wage.

Workers will not know the identity of their employer and likewise the employers will not know the identity of the worker. The overall results of the hiring stage are however communicated to everyone: that includes information on how many were hired and at what wages.

Work stage

The hired workers have 5 minutes to work. They can attempt at most {{ Constants.max_task_amount }} counting tasks. A task consists of two boxes, each containing 100 numbers. The job is to find the biggest number in each of the two boxes and then add the two numbers together. Each correctly completed task gives the employer a return of 20 points. Wrong answers are worthless but count as attempted tasks. Workers can submit one answer per task.

Payoffs

After the 5 minute period or the maximum of 10 tasks, all participants are directed to the results page. The worker and employer hiring will get to know the number of tasks attempted and tasks correct by the worker and the subsequent final payoffs of each other, but they will not learn this information for other participants.

If the participant does not have a contract, the payoffs are the following:

Employer’s payoff = 0 points

Worker’s payoff = 20 points

If the participant has a contract:

Employer’s payoff = 40 - wage + 20 * number of correct tasks

Worker’s payoff = wage

In words, the employer receives a 40 points when hiring a worker, pays out the wage, and receives revenue from correctly completed tasks. What remains is the payoff of the employer and note that the payoff can also be negative. A hired worker's payoff consists of the wage.
