Purpose

You are being asked to be a volunteer in a research study. This study is conducted by Professor Jeffrey Hales (Georgia Institute of Technology) and Jordan Samet (Georgia Institute of Technology). Its purpose is to examine how people make decisions. We intend to recruit up to 200 to 500 subjects.

Procedures

If you agree to participate in this study, you will first be randomly assigned to your role in the study and then presented with rules to the first stage. You will then participate in the first stage. This participation will vary depending on your randomly assigned role. Afterwards, you will be presented with the rules to the second stage of the study, according to your assigned role. You will then participate in the second stage. As before, your participation will vary depending on your randomly assigned role. Lastly, you will be asked to complete a survey including demographic questions. The experiment will take approximately 40 minutes of your time.

Risks/Discomforts

We do not expect that you will encounter any risks or discomforts from participating in this study.

Benefits & Costs

Your participation will help us learn more about how people make decisions under various situations. Understanding how people make decisions in response to different information is the first step in understanding how decision-making might be improved.

Confidentiality

The researchers will make every effort to protect your privacy. Your responses will be associated with a code number that we assign, but that number is not and will not be connected in any way with your name. In order to receive payment, you will be given a random code at the beginning of the study. Once you have completed the study you will bring your code to the lab attendant who will provide you your payment, based on the code, prior to your departure. Thus, your responses are anonymous. The data will only be accessible to the researchers. In any presentations, written reports or publications, only aggregated results will be presented. Your privacy will be protected to the extent required by law.. You should be aware that the experiment is not being run from a secure https server of the kind typically used to handle credit card transactions, so there is a small possibility that responses could be viewed by unauthorized third parties such as computer hackers. In general, the web page software will log as header lines the IP address of the machine you use to access this page, e.g.,102.403.506.807, but otherwise no other information will be stored unless you explicitly enter it.
To make sure that this research is being carried out in the proper way, the Georgia Institute of Technology Institutional Review Board may review study records. The Office of Human Research Protections may also look at study records.

Inclusion/Exclusion Criteria

To participate in this study, you must meet the following two criteria: (1) you must be at least 18 years of age and (2) you must be relatively fluent in the English language.

Compensation

You will receive a payment of $5.00 for participating in this study. In addition, you will receive a bonus, depending on your actions and decisions as well as on the actions and decisions of other study participants. We expect that you should be able to complete this task in around 40 minutes. If you decide at any point that you do not wish to continue participating, you may simply stop by closing the browser window and leaving the experimental lab. You will only receive compensation if you complete the study.

In Case of Injury/Harm

If you are injured as a result of being in this study, please contact Dr. Jeffrey Hales at 404-894-3897. Neither Georgia Institute of Technology nor the experimenters have made provision for payment of costs associated with any injury resulting from participation in this study.

Participant Rights

  • Your participation in this study is strictly voluntary
  • You have the right to change your mind and leave the study at any time without giving reason
  • You do not waive any of your legal rights by providing your consent
  • You do not have to be in this study if you don't want to be. You have the right to change your mind and leave the study at any time without giving any reason and without penalty

Questions about the Research

If you have any questions about the study, please contact one of the researchers using the following information:
  • Jeffrey Hales, Professor, Georgia Institute of Technology, 404-894-3897, jeff.hales@scheller.gatech.edu.
  • Jordan Samet, PhD Student, Georgia Institute of Technology, 404-894-9474, jordan.samet@scheller.gatech.edu.

Questions about your Rights as Research Participants

If you have any questions about your rights as a research participant, you may contact Ms. Melanie Clark, Georgia Institute of Technology, Office of Research Integrity Assurance, at 404-894-6942.
If you complete the task and survey on the computer terminal in front of you, it means that you have read (or have had read to you) the information contained in this letter, and you would like to be a volunteer in this study.
